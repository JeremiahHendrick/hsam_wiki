Overview

Hey Stop A Minute (commonly referred to as “Hey Stop A Minute”) is a creature created by Canadian horror artist Trevor Henderson. It appears in a single, well-known image depicting a tall, eerie humanoid form peering over a fence while calling out to an unseen passerby.

The creature’s name is derived from the caption associated with the artwork, in which it appears to say the phrase “hey stop a minute”. Beyond this image and line of dialogue, there is little to no officially released lore from the creator, leaving the entity’s nature and intentions unclear.

Appearance

In the original artwork, the entity is seen from behind a fence or barrier, with only its upper body visible. Its exact height is not specified, but it appears significantly taller than a human adult based on the framing of the image.

Key visual traits include:

  • A tall, lanky humanoid silhouette visible from the chest or shoulders upward.
  • A smooth, hairless head with dark or shadowy skin.
  • Large, deep-set, and unnaturally dark eyes; one eye appears larger than the other.
  • Dark nostrils and a mouth with an almost completely black interior.
  • A partially obscured torso, with the lower body hidden behind the fence, leaving its full shape unknown.

Due to the limited view and stylized nature of the art, details such as its exact proportions, limbs, or any non-human body features remain ambiguous. Some fans have speculated that the body may be distorted or animal-like, but such ideas are not supported by direct canon.

Behavior

The only clearly documented behavior of the creature is its act of calling out to someone nearby with the phrase “hey stop a minute”. This suggests that it is capable of speech or vocal mimicry and is aware of a human presence beyond the fence.

Since Henderson has not provided further description, its motives are unclear. The call could be interpreted as:

  • a request for attention,
  • a lure to bring someone closer,
  • or a simple, unsettling greeting with unknown intent.

Any depictions of the creature chasing people, attacking them, or exhibiting complex behavior patterns originate from fan media such as videos and game modifications and are not part of official lore.

Abilities

No explicit supernatural abilities have been confirmed by the creator. The artwork only implies:

  • The creature possesses a voice or sound-producing ability.
  • It has enough awareness to address someone and request that they stop.

Popular fan portrayals often assign additional powers—such as high speed, physical strength, teleportation, or reality distortion—to fit horror gameplay or narrative needs. These abilities, while common in community content, should be treated as non-canonical.

Origin and context

As with many of Trevor Henderson’s creations, Hey Stop A Minute has no publicly detailed origin story. There is no official information about:

  • where it came from,
  • whether it was once human,
  • its age or “birth,”
  • or its purpose or goals.

The image uses a familiar, almost mundane environment (a simple fence and background), combined with an unnatural figure, evoking a liminal-horror feeling: something wrong appearing in an otherwise ordinary scene.

Presence in fan media

Despite having very little official lore, the creature has gained notable popularity in fan communities, especially through Garry’s Mod and other sandbox games. It frequently appears as:

  • a nextbot or scripted NPC that chases players,
  • a horror-themed antagonist in custom maps or videos,
  • a background cryptid in compilation-style “Trevor Henderson creatures” videos.

These portrayals often expand its size, speed, and aggressiveness to create more dramatic or frightening scenarios. While such depictions are widely viewed, they represent community-driven interpretation rather than official characterization.

Canon status and misinformation

Due to the open nature of fan wikis and community edits, various pieces of misinformation have appeared online regarding Hey Stop A Minute. These can include claims about:

  • its “gender” or “sexuality,”
  • specific birthdays (such as real-world dates),
  • extensive personality profiles,
  • detailed power sets or combat feats.

There is no evidence that Trevor Henderson has assigned the creature a gender identity, sexuality, exact birthday, or personal relationships. Such details should be considered either headcanon or vandalism on editable wikis, not accepted canon.

In summary, canon information about the entity is limited to:

  • its visual design as seen in the original artwork,
  • its position behind a fence,
  • and the phrase “hey stop a minute,” directed at someone off-screen.
